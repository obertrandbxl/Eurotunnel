COVID-19 Travel Requirements

Both the UK and French governments have introduced additional entry requirements and restrictions due to COVID-19. Please remember, it is your responsibility to ensure you check and comply with the latest health and travel regulations. Anyone not meeting travel regulations or failing to produce the required documentation for their journey will not be permitted to travel.

Travel from the UK to France
Travel from France to the UK

We are open and our service is running

However, due to the need for all passengers to comply with the COVID-19 travel requirements specified by the UK and FR governments, all Le Shuttle bookings must be made at least 24 hours in advance. It is no longer possible to purchase a ticket at check-in.

Last updated: 02/09/2021

Travel from the UK to France

We do our best to interpret and update this page as soon as advice from the French government changes. For the very latest advice on entering France, we recommend checking diplomatie.gouv.fr, interieur.gouv.fr and uk.ambafrance.org before travel.

Vaccinated Travellers

1. Sworn statement of honour
Fully vaccinated passengers are only required to complete a "Statement of honour"

  • Download from interieur.gouv.fr
    (Click "TRAVEL TO/FROM AN AMBER LIST COUNTRY")

This must be completed for each passenger, and presented digitally or printed to border control authorities.

Fully vaccinated passengers, and passengers under 12 years old, will not be required to present negative COVID tests to enter French territory, but proof of vaccination status will need to be presented at border controls. 

You will not be required to quarantine upon arrival in France. Unvaccinated minors travelling with fully vaccinated adults will not be required to quarantine.

Important: Prior to travel, you can upload the required document above to your booking by logging into your account/booking and completing your Health Declarations. Failure to do so will result in manual checks being required which could delay your departure.

Complete this step now

Help completing your Health Declarations | Help logging in | Travel agent booking log-in

Unvaccinated Travellers

1. French health declarations
Unvaccinated passengers are required to supply 2 documents

  • a "Statement of honour" - required for all passengers 12 and over
  • a "Travel certificate to Metropolitan France" - required for all passengers 18 and over.

Download both documents from interieur.gouv.fr
(Click "TRAVEL TO/FROM AN AMBER LIST COUNTRY")

These must be completed for each passenger, and presented digitally or printed to border control authorities.

2. Negative COVID tests
Unvaccinated passengers over 12 years old require a negative result from a non-NHS PCR test or a non-NHS Antigen (lateral flow) test taken no more than 24 hours before travel. Find a private test provider.

At French border controls you will need to present digital or paper evidence of the negative result from the private test provider.

3. Quarantine
Unvaccinated passengers over 12 years old must quarantine for 7 days upon arrival in France (unless transiting through France in under 24hrs). Unvaccinated minors travelling with fully vaccinated adults will not be required to quarantine.

Important: Prior to travel, you can upload the required documents/results above to your booking by logging into your account/booking and completing your Health Declarations. Failure to do so will result in manual checks being required which could delay your departure.

Log in and upload documents now

Help completing your Health Declarations | Help logging in | Travel agent booking log-in

Travel from France to the UK

We do our best to interpret and update this page as soon as advice from the UK government changes. For the very latest advice on entering the UK, we recommend checking gov.uk before travel.

Important: All passengers must follow the rules and requirements for travelling from an Amber list country, even if transiting through France from a Green list country to the UK.

Fully Vaccinated Travellers

1. A negative COVID test
All passengers aged 11 or over travelling on our passenger service require a negative result from a non-NHS PCR or a non-NHS Antigen (lateral flow). You must take the test in the 3 days before your booked departure. 

At UK border controls you will need to present an email or text message certificate in English, French or Spanish issued by the testing facility (more information) - failure to do so will result in a £500 fine.

  • Find a private test provider in France

There is no COVID-19 testing facility at the Eurotunnel site. It's your responsibility to ensure the test meets the minimum standards for sensitivity, specificity and viral load details.

2. A "Day 2" COVID test
Fully vaccinated passengers must also purchase/book a travel testing package prior to travel (check rules for children and young people). After arrival, you must take a coronavirus (COVID-19) test on or before the second day. The "test pack" must be booked for each passenger (children aged 4 and under do not need to take these tests) before you begin your trip and its reference number must be specified on your passenger locator form before travelling. Details can be found here.

  • Choose a test provider

3. Passenger Locator Form
All passengers aged 18 or over must complete an individual passenger locator form before arrival at the terminal in France (details of children under 18 must be included on the form completed by the adult accompanying them). Each passenger locator form must specify the unique reference number of the booked pre-travel testing package. This form must be presented in digital or paper format to the border authorities.

  • Complete the passenger locator form online

Each passenger must bring ALL the above documents and supporting evidence with them, ready to present to the authorities.

Important: Prior to travel, you can upload the required documents/results above to your booking, by logging into your account/booking and completing your Health Declarations. Failure to do so will result in manual checks being required which could delay your departure.

Log in and upload documents now

Help completing your Health Declarations | Help logging in | Travel agent booking log-in

Quarantine

  • Fully vaccinated passengers, and passengers under 18 years old, will not be required to quarantine upon arrival in the UK.

Failure to comply with the above measures or to provide false or deliberately misleading information is a criminal offence and is punishable by a fine and/or travel ban and/or imprisonment. Please visit www.gov.uk/uk-border-control for detailed public health advice and requirements for entering the UK.

Unvaccinated Travellers

1. A negative COVID test
All passengers aged 11 or over travelling on our passenger service require a negative result from a non-NHS PCR or a non-NHS Antigen (lateral flow). You must take the test in the 3 days before your booked departure.

At UK border controls you will need to present an email or text message certificate in English, French or Spanish issued by the testing facility (more information) - failure to do so will result in a £500 fine.

  • Find a private test provider in France

There is no COVID-19 testing facility at the Eurotunnel site. It's your responsibility to ensure the test meets the minimum standards for sensitivity, specificity and viral load details.

2. A 2 & 8 day COVID test package
Unvaccinated passengers must also purchase/book a travel testing package prior to travel (check rules for children and young people). You must take a coronavirus (COVID-19) test on second and eighth day of quarantine. The "test pack" must be booked for each passenger (children aged 4 and under do not need to take these tests) before you begin your trip and its reference number must be specified on your passenger locator form before travelling. Details can be found here.

  • Choose a test provider

3. Passenger Locator Form
All passengers aged 18 or over must complete an individual passenger locator form before arrival at the terminal in France (details of children under 18 must be included on the form completed by the adult accompanying them). Each passenger locator form must specify the unique reference number of the booked pre-travel testing package. This form must be presented in digital or paper format to the border authorities.

  • Complete the passenger locator form online

Each passenger must bring ALL the above documents and supporting evidence with them, ready to present to the authorities.

Important: Prior to travel, you can upload the required documents/results above to your booking, by logging into your account/booking and completing your Health Declarations. Failure to do so will result in manual checks being required which could delay your departure.

Quarantine

  • All unvaccinated arrivals must quarantine for 10 days unless exempt. You may opt into Test to Release.

Failure to comply with the above measures or to provide false or deliberately misleading information is a criminal offence and is punishable by a fine and/or travel ban and/or imprisonment. Please visit www.gov.uk/uk-border-control for detailed public health advice and requirements for entering the UK.

Terminal Buildings and Shuttles

Our terminals are open during the day, but many facilities are unavailable due to Government guidelines. Toilets and vending machines are also available in the boarding lanes at both our Folkestone and Calais terminals. Please be aware that customers must remain in their vehicles on the shuttle so toilets on board are closed.

Eurotunnel travel insurance

Our Covid-19 Travel insurance and Combined products now include cover for cancellation prior to travel. However, it is IMPORTANT to note that there is no travel insurance cover (including for curtailment, quarantine and medical expenses) whilst overseas, if the FCDO advises against all but essential travel in the country you are travelling to. European Motor Breakdown cover is not affected by any travel restrictions. Please click here for Frequently asked Questions.

International travel rules

France is currently on the Amber list. For a full list of rules you must follow when travelling to, through or from an Amber list country, please see GOV.uk for more information.

Pre-travel testing FAQs

  • Can I travel if vaccinated with the Indian AstraZeneca Vaccine?

    There is currently no information or advice from any government agencies to suggest that you will be blocked from travelling or be subject to different quarantine rules. 

  • The entry requirements for the UK/France changed since I last checked

    Each countries governments are responsible for the setting their own specific entry requirements, and can change them at any time without warning.

    Eurotunnel is not responsible for these rules and entry requirements. 

    We do our best to interpret and update our COVID-19 page as soon as advice from the French or UK governments change.

    • uk.ambafrance.org

    • gov.uk

    Yes - First you must add your Advance Passenger Information, and then add your COVID Health Declarations.

    COVID-19 testing for the purposes of international travel is not available on the NHS and are not accepted by the border authorities.

    Please check carefully as anyone not meeting the criteria will not be permitted to travel.

    Please note there is NO COVID-19 testing facility at Eurotunnel.

FAQs

  • Can I extend my Frequent Traveller tickets/wallets due to COVID related disruption?

    If you have a Frequent Traveller Wallet which is due to expire, we can confirm they will be automatically extended to 30th June 2022, when all travel must be completed.

    The updated expiry date will show in your wallet in the next few weeks.

    Our phone lines are extremely busy – please only contact us if you really need to.

  • Can I transit through France to another country?

    Yes, although you will still be subject to requirements. 

    When filling out the "Travel certificate to Metropolitan France", point 11 "Traveller in transit in the international zone for less than 24 hours" is listed as a valid reason for travel.

    More information is available here: https://www.interieur.gouv.fr/Actualites/L-actu-du-Ministere/Certificate-of-international-travel

    All information regarding quarantine rules can be found on the link above - unfortunately we cannot provide any further information.
  • Can I request a Credit Voucher to replace a non-refundable ticket due to COVID related disruption?

    As the lock-down continues, and Government restrictions stay in place and you have an unused booking that was made before 31/12/2020, we are pleased exceptionally to be able to offer you the option of requesting a credit valid for redemption 12 months from the date of issue.

    Click here to request a Credit Voucher

    Please note that Credit Vouchers cannot be used online at this time.

  • Can I extend the validity of Credit Voucher issued due to COVID related disruption?

    As the lock-down continues, and Government restrictions stay in place we are pleased exceptionally, to be able to offer you the option of requesting a replacement for your original credit voucher, issued before 31/12/2020 allowing a further 12 month (from issue) to redeem.

    Click here to request an extension

    Please note that Credit Vouchers cannot be used online at this time.

  • I no longer want to travel, can I re-book or get a refund?

    Refundable tickets:
    If you have purchased a ticket that allows cancellations such as a “Standard Refundable” ticket, Frequent Traveller or Flexiplus ticket, please complete this form to submit a cancellation request.

    Non-refundable tickets:
    Most tickets can be amended online by clicking here. You can amend multiple times without amendment fees. If you are unable to amend your ticket online, please don’t worry if you can’t get through to us on the phone. 

    Bookings made with Tesco or other discount vouchers
    If you have purchased a ticket in full or part using a Tesco or other discount voucher, please contact us by phone to amend your booking.

  • Has Eurotunnel put additional measures in place?

    Eurotunnel has implemented additional cleaning procedures. Automatic hand sanitisers have been installed in passenger areas in both the UK and France, toilet facilities are being cleaned and checked for supplies more frequently, and all cleaning regimes have been increased on the terminals, and onboard the shuttles.

     

     

    The Visit England “We’re Good To Go” Industry Standard

    This UK-wide industry standard and supporting mark demonstrates that Eurotunnel fully adheres to the respective government and public health guidance, has carried out a COVID-19 risk assessment and checked all required processes have been implemented.

     

    AFNOR (Association Française de Normalisation) Certification

    AFNOR has awarded Eurotunnel with the highest score of 100% for COVID-19 compliant practices. The certification, which was given following a two-day audit, recognises the efficacy of all the measures we’ve put in place to ensure the safety and protection of customers and staff.

  • What happens if the borders close?

    We continue to carefully monitor official Government advice here in the UK and France.

    If you have questions regarding possible future changes to travel through and to France or the rest of Europe; Please do not call our Contact Centre, or contact us by email. We are unable to offer any advice or information other than what is available from the Foreign & Commonwealth Office. See here for more information.

  • I think I have been in contact with someone who has been affected, can I travel?

    Please follow the official travel and health advice issued by your country’s travel and health authorities.

  • I have been advised to self-isolate and am due to travel, what do I do?

    If you have been advised to self-isolate and have a booking that falls within the dates of your isolation period, please contact us directly and we will be happy to assist with exchanging your booking for a future date within the validity of your ticket.  If you have booked on-line you can amend your booking here: My Eurotunnel
